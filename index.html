I'm a particle physicist currently based at University of Wisconsin-Madison working mainly on the NOvA and DUNE experiments. My research focuses primarily on sterile neutrino searches and development of liquid argon detectors.

research

I currently work as part of the NOvA and DUNE collaborations

NOvA is the current flagship experiment of the Fermi National Accelerator Laboratory. The primary goal of the experiment is to make precision measurements of neutrino oscillations.
DUNE is the next-generation neutrino oscillation experiment, which will expand the reach of NOvA with much larger event counts, allowing even more precise measurements such as CP violation.

Previously, I completed my PhD while working on the MicroBooNE experiment. MicroBooNE is a short-baseline experiment trying to verify or refute a set of anomalous results from the last two decades.
